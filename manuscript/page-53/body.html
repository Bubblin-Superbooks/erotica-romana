Ah, to uphold one's respectable name is not easy. The Lady Fame has an ancient foe: Cupid, my master and lord. Oh, by the way, have you heard of the cause of their mutual hatred? It's an old story, I think—Let me just tell it again. Powerful ever the goddess, but nevertheless to her fellows Overbearing and rude, quite unendurable. She Had by the gods since time out of mind at their banquets been dreaded, Yelling with brassiest voice orders to great and to small. Once, in her arrogance even maintained that she had subjected To her own will, as her slave, Jove's most illustrious son. "One of these days, O father of deities," cried she in triumph, "I shall be bringing you my—Hercules, as if new born.
